Hypothesis Validation

Six hypotheses tested against 1,815 DE responses. All confirmed.

H1 Confirmed

Strategy exists but doesn't drive action

71% have a strategy, but ~40% cannot confidently measure impact. 72.4% still in maturity stages 1-2.

~40% cannot measure L&D impact
H2 Confirmed

Culture fails on structure, not motivation

Active culture gap: 23pp. Feedback translates to plans: 28pp gap. Empowerment gap: 26pp. Culture gaps are wider in DE than NL.

28pp feedback-to-development gap
H3 Confirmed

Manager perception gap

Design learning paths gap: 26pp. Skill clarity gap: 25pp. Proactive upskilling gap: 16pp. DE shows wider gaps than NL across all support dimensions.

26pp learning path design gap
H4 Confirmed

Skill transparency crisis

Only 10% have full skill/gap transparency. 32.4% say goals not defined along skills. Manager vs employee skill clarity gap: 24.7pp.

NETHERLANDS

Narrower perception gaps across the board — feedback→development 17pp (vs 28pp DE), skill clarity 15pp (vs 25pp DE). More optimistic investment outlook (+2.3pp increasing). More ambitious 3-year outlook (68% aspire beyond vs 33% DE).

GERMANY

Slightly higher strategy adoption and skill transparency. But significantly wider perception gaps across all dimensions: feedback→development 28pp (vs 17pp NL), skill clarity 25pp (vs 15pp NL), empowerment 26pp (vs 8pp NL). Facing more investment pressure (14.4% declining). More modest ambitions — 42% just want standardization.
